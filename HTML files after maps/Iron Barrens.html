War For Bramnunea

Iron Barrens — Mines, Metal, and the Bones of the Planet



The Iron Barrens are a region of iron‑rich desert where the sands shimmer with metallic dust and the ground rings hollow beneath one’s feet. The entire region is riddled with mines — some active, others abandoned, many forgotten. Mining towns cling to the edges of open pits, their populations a mix of labourers, exiles, and fortune‑seekers hoping to strike a vein of high‑grade ore. The air tastes of rust, and the wind carries the metallic tang of exposed iron.
The Barrens are dangerous even without the mines. Sinkholes, toxic dust storms, and unstable caverns claim lives regularly. Yet the region remains vital to the planet’s industry, supplying the metals needed for construction, weaponry, and machinery. The deeper mines are rumoured to contain relics of ancient conflicts — shattered armour, buried engines, and tunnels that predate Imperial settlement.



After the Rift




The Rift’s opening caused seismic instability across the Iron Barrens. Mines collapsed without warning, and strange magnetic anomalies disrupted equipment. Some miners reported hearing rhythmic pounding deep underground, as if something massive were moving far below. Others claimed to see rust‑coloured lights flickering in abandoned shafts. The Mechanicus declared several sites off‑limits, though rumours persist of expeditions continuing in secret. In the age of Nihilus, the Iron Barrens are the rusted heart of the desert — dangerous, essential, and full of secrets best left buried.
