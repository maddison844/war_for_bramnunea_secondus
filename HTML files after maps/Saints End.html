War For Bramnunea

Saint’s End— Where the Saint Fell and the World Was Reborn



Saint’s End is a harsh, windswept region of badlands, scrub plains, and jagged hills. A single great river flows southward through the territory, carving a lifeline of green through an otherwise unforgiving landscape. It was here, during the reclamation of Branunea Secondus, that Saint Biffobrin fell in battle against heretic warlords — an act of sacrifice that paved the way for the planet’s return to Imperial rule.
The site of the saint’s death is marked by a monumental shrine‑fortress, built atop the very ground where he made his last stand. Pilgrims travel from across the world to walk the Path of Martyrdom, a winding trail that traces the saint’s final hours. The region’s settlements are sparse and rugged, their people shaped by the land’s severity and the weight of its history.



After the Rift




When the Great Rift opened, Saint’s End became a place of ominous significance. Warp‑charged storms rolled across the badlands, and the river glowed faintly under the auroras. Pilgrims reported visions — some inspiring, others terrifying. The shrine‑fortress became both sanctuary and stronghold, its priests interpreting the Rift’s signs as tests of faith. The people of Saint’s End believe the saint watches over them still, and that his martyrdom has bound the region to the Emperor in ways deeper than scripture can express. In the age of Nihilus, Saint’s End stands as a holy scar, a reminder that sacrifice is the price of survival.
