War For Bramnunea

Redemption’s Price — Lumber, Loss, and the Cost of Survival



Redemption’s Price lies west of Saint’s End, marking the point where the Central Frontier’s scrublands give way to the first true reaches of the Western Marches’ jungle. Once, this region was part of the vast forest that covered nearly the entire planet. Now, it is a landscape of stumps, logging camps, stripped hillsides, and industrial scars. Massive lumber operations dominate the region, feeding the hive‑city in the Valley of Judgement and supplying construction materials for the entire planet.
The region’s name is a grim reminder of the cost of survival: the planet’s prosperity was bought with the destruction of its ancient forests. Many in the Marches resent the deforestation, seeing it as a wound inflicted upon their homeland. Others view it as necessary — a sacrifice made so that Branunea Secondus could grow, build, and defend itself.



After the Rift




The Rift’s opening brought both opportunity and danger to Redemption’s Price. Warp‑storms disrupted machinery, and strange growths appeared in the remaining forest, resisting attempts to cut them down. Some lumber crews vanished entirely, their camps found abandoned with tools still in hand. Yet the demand for lumber increased as the planet fortified itself against isolation. The region became a battleground between industry and the wilds, with the forest seeming to fight back. In the age of Nihilus, Redemption’s Price is the scar that refuses to heal, a reminder that survival always demands a price.
