War For Bramnunea

South Vale — The Frontier’s Uneasy Threshold



The South Vale is a region of hilly terrain where the fertile lands of the Central Frontier begin to give way to the arid badlands beyond. Its landscape is a patchwork of grassy slopes, rocky outcrops, and narrow valleys carved by seasonal streams. Farming here is difficult but not impossible; hardy crops and goat herds dominate the region’s economy, and its people are known for their adaptability and toughness.
The South Vale has long served as a buffer zone, protecting the richer lands to the north from raiders, dust storms, and the unpredictable dangers of the badlands. Its settlements are fortified, its militia well‑trained, and its culture shaped by vigilance. The region’s people are fiercely independent, often acting as guides, scouts, or caravan guards for those travelling deeper into the frontier.



After the Rift




The Rift’s opening intensified the South Vale’s challenges. Weather patterns became erratic, with scorching heat followed by sudden cold snaps. Strange lights flickered across the hills at night, and travellers reported hearing whispers on the wind. The region’s militias doubled their patrols, and many villages built new watchtowers to guard against threats both mundane and supernatural. Yet the South Vale endured, becoming the first line of defence against whatever the age of Nihilus might bring.
