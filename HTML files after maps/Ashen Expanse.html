War For Bramnunea

Ashen Expanse — Endless Sand and the Silence of the Dead



The Ashen Expanse is the purest desert on Branunea Secondus — a vast sea of fine, pale sand stretching from the Iron Barrens to the western border near Saint’s End. The dunes shift constantly, sculpted by relentless winds into towering ridges and deep hollows. Nothing grows here; nothing survives for long. Caravans avoid the region unless absolutely necessary, and even then only with experienced guides who know how to read the subtle language of the dunes.
The Expanse is a place of legend. Some say entire cities lie buried beneath the sands, swallowed by storms in ages past. Others speak of creatures that move beneath the dunes, hunting by vibration. The region’s silence is absolute — no birds, no insects, no life. Only the wind, and the whisper of shifting sand.



After the Rift




The Rift’s opening turned the Ashen Expanse into a place of unnatural stillness. The winds died for days at a time, leaving the dunes frozen in place. Then, without warning, storms erupted with terrifying force, reshaping the landscape overnight. Travellers reported seeing figures standing atop distant dunes — silhouettes that vanished when approached. The Expanse’s sands began to glow faintly under the auroras, creating ghostly patterns visible only at night. In the age of Nihilus, the Ashen Expanse is the silent sea, a place where the boundary between desert and dream has all but dissolved.
