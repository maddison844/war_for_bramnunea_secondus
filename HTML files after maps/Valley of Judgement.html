War For Bramnunea

Valley of Judgement— Industry, Isolation, and the Weight of History



The Valley of Judgement is a vast, river‑cut basin carved by a great north‑flowing river that winds through the heart of the Western Marches. It is one of the few open regions in the province, and thus the only place where a hive‑city could be built. The hive rises from the valley floor like a steel monolith, its lower levels surrounded by dense forest and its upper spires piercing the mist. It is a city of industry, processing lumber, minerals, and biomass harvested from the surrounding wilderness.
The valley’s name comes from an ancient pre‑Imperial tradition: criminals and exiles were once cast into the forested basin and left to survive or perish. When the Imperium reclaimed the world, the hive was built atop these old execution grounds, transforming a place of death into a centre of production. Yet the name endured, and many say the forest remembers the blood spilled there.



After the Rift




When the Rift opened, the Valley of Judgement became a fortress. Warp‑storms disrupted the river’s flow, causing floods and droughts in unpredictable cycles. The hive’s lower levels were besieged by mutated wildlife and forest predators driven from their habitats by strange disturbances. The upper spires flickered with static as the auroras above the Rift danced across the sky. Yet the hive endured, sealing its lower gates and reinforcing its walls. In the age of Nihilus, the Valley of Judgement is the iron heart of the Western Marches — a bastion of industry standing defiantly against the encroaching wilds.
