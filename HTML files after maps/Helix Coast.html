War For Bramnunea

Helix Coast — Cliffs, Storms, and the Sea That Devours



The Helix Coast is a brutal stretch of coastline defined by jagged cliffs, treacherous reefs, and waters prone to violent, unpredictable weather. Towering waves crash against the stone, carving spiralling formations that gave the region its name. Storms roll in without warning, turning the sea into a churning cauldron capable of swallowing ships whole. Few dare to sail these waters, and those who do are either desperate, brave, or foolish.
Inland, the coast gives way almost immediately to the dense, choking forests that dominate the Western Marches. Settlements cling to cliff edges or perch on rocky plateaus, their people hardened by generations of battling the sea and the jungle alike. The Helix Coast is known for its storm‑watchers, lighthouse‑fortresses, and the grim tradition of carving the names of the drowned into the cliff face — a memorial constantly threatened by the waves that claimed them.



After the Rift




The opening of the Great Rift transformed the Helix Coast into a place of near‑constant fury. Warp‑charged storms intensified the already violent weather, creating lightning displays that danced across the sea like living things. Entire sections of cliff collapsed into the ocean, and strange, phosphorescent tides washed ashore. Fishermen reported seeing ghostly shapes beneath the waves, and some claimed the storms whispered in human voices. The coast’s lighthouse‑fortresses became critical watchpoints, scanning both sea and sky for threats. In the age of Nihilus, the Helix Coast stands as the first warning line, where the planet’s storms and the galaxy’s madness meet.
