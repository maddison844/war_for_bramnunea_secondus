War For Bramnunea

Tithlands — Fields of Endless Obligation



The Tithlands are the agricultural engine of Branunea Secondus. Stretching across vast plains irrigated by ancient canal networks, the region produces more food than any other part of the planet. Its name comes from its original purpose: to grow crops specifically for the Imperial tithe. For centuries, Administratum overseers monitored every harvest, every grain count, every shipment. Entire townships were built around tithe‑silos, processing stations, and rail lines that once carried food north to Widow’s Coast for off‑world transport.
Life in the Tithlands is defined by scale. Fields stretch to the horizon, worked by massive agri‑tractors and teams of bonded labourers. The region’s settlements are utilitarian and regimented, built to house workers efficiently rather than comfortably. Yet despite the harshness of their labour, the people of the Tithlands take pride in their role. They see themselves as the providers, the ones who keep the world fed and the Imperium supplied.



After the Rift




With the tithe routes severed, the Tithlands underwent a profound transformation. No longer bound by Administratum quotas, the region redirected its output to sustain Branunea Secondus itself. But the Rift brought challenges: warp‑storms disrupted irrigation systems, strange blights affected crops, and the night sky glowed with colours that unsettled livestock. The Tithlands responded with characteristic discipline — doubling shifts, reinforcing canal networks, and storing surplus grain in fortified silos. In the age of Nihilus, the Tithlands have become the shield of sustenance, feeding a world cut off from the Imperium.
