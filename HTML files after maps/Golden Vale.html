Golden Vale - Fertile, Faithful, and Forever Overshadowed



The Golden Vale is a region of rolling fields, soft hills, and warm, sun‑washed plains. Its soil is fertile, though not as rich or intensively cultivated as the Tithlands to the east. The Vale’s farmers often joke that their land “grows enough to live, but not enough to boast,” and this humility has shaped the region’s culture. Villages here are small but tightly knit, built around communal granaries, old stone chapels, and market greens where seasonal fairs bring the scattered communities together.
The Vale’s name comes not from its productivity but from the golden hue that sweeps across its fields at dawn and dusk — a natural beauty that has inspired poets, painters, and pilgrims for centuries. The region is known for its orchards, its hardy grain varieties, and its tradition of producing skilled horse‑handlers and scouts. Though overshadowed by the Tithlands’ tithe‑scale output, the Golden Vale is considered the soul of the Central Frontier — a place where the land is worked with reverence rather than obligation.



After the Rift




The opening of the Great Rift brought unpredictable weather to the Vale. Sudden frosts, unseasonal rains, and shimmering warp‑lights in the sky disrupted planting cycles. Yet the Vale adapted with quiet determination. Farmers began experimenting with hybrid crops, and the region’s chapels became centres of communal resilience. Some claim that the golden light of the Vale has grown brighter since the Rift — a sign, they say, that the Emperor still watches over them. Whether miracle or myth, the Golden Vale has become a symbol of hope in a darkened galaxy.
