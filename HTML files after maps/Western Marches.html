Western Marches




The Wild Frontier — Forest, Fury, and the Edge of the Known World
The Western Marches are the most untamed and dangerous province of Branunea Secondus — a land where civilisation clings to the edges of vast, ancient wilderness. The Marches stretch from the jagged, storm‑lashed coastline of the Helix Coast to the endless steppe of Terra Terminus, with dense, primeval forests filling everything in between. These forests once covered nearly the entire planet, but centuries of industrial strip‑harvesting have reduced them to a fraction of their former glory. What remains is a colossal, humid, choking expanse of vegetation — a living world unto itself, teeming with predators, poisonous flora, and secrets older than Imperial rule.
The people of the Western Marches are shaped by this environment. They are hardy, suspicious of outsiders, and fiercely protective of their land. Settlements cling to rivers, cliff edges, and clearings carved by generations of lumber crews. The Marches are also home to the Valley of Judgement, the only hive‑city in the province — a towering industrial bastion built in a rare open valley carved by a great north‑flowing river. The hive stands as both a symbol of Imperial dominance and a reminder of what was lost: the forests that once stretched unbroken to the horizon.



After the Rift




When the Great Rift tore open, the Western Marches were plunged into chaos. Warp‑charged storms intensified the already brutal weather, and the great forests seemed to grow darker, stranger, and more hostile. Lumber operations faltered as machinery malfunctioned and workers vanished in the mist. The hive‑city in the Valley of Judgement became a fortress against both natural and unnatural threats. Yet the Marches endured. Their people had always lived on the edge of survival, and in the age of Nihilus, they became the planet’s shield against the unknown, standing between Branunea Secondus and whatever horrors might emerge from the deep woods or the storm‑wracked coast.
