War For Bramnunea

Helican Tract — Dunes, Cliffs, and the Drowned Frontier



The Helican Tract is a long stretch of northern desert that slopes downward until it meets the ocean in a series of eroded cliffs and shifting beaches. Here, the dunes roll like waves, descending into the surf in great golden sweeps. The coastline is treacherous — sandbars shift constantly, and the sea is prone to violent storms that hurl salt spray far inland. The region’s settlements cling to rocky outcrops or cluster around rare freshwater springs, their people hardened by generations of living between two unforgiving worlds.
The Tract is known for its salt‑caravans, long trains of beasts and crawlers that harvest mineral deposits from the cliffs and tidal flats. Ancient wrecks lie half‑buried along the shore, remnants of pre‑Imperial vessels that attempted to navigate the shifting sands and failed. The region’s culture blends desert nomad traditions with coastal survivalism, producing hardy sailors, dune‑riders, and scavengers who know how to read both wind and tide.



After the Rift




The Rift’s opening brought violent tidal surges and warp‑lit storms to the Helican Tract. Entire sections of coastline collapsed into the sea, and strange phosphorescent algae washed ashore, glowing faintly in the night. Caravans reported mirages that persisted even when approached, and some sailors claimed the ocean whispered beneath the waves. The Tract’s people adapted, as they always have, reinforcing their cliffside settlements and learning to navigate by the unnatural auroras. In the age of Nihilus, the Helican Tract stands as the shifting threshold between land, sea, and the unknown.
