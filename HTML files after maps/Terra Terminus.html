War For Bramnunea

Terra Terminus — Endless Steppe and the Silence Beyond



Terra Terminus is a vast, windswept steppe that stretches westward until the land simply ends. To stand on its farthest reaches is to feel as though one is staring into infinity. The terrain is flat, open, and eerily quiet — a stark contrast to the dense forests that dominate the rest of the province. Nomadic herders, frontier militias, and wandering traders traverse the steppe, following ancient routes marked by stone cairns and weather‑worn shrines.
The region’s name reflects its character: it feels like the end of the world, a place where civilisation thins and the wilds reclaim their dominion. Storms sweep across the plains with terrifying speed, and the night sky is so clear that the stars seem close enough to touch. Terra Terminus has always been a place of solitude, reflection, and danger.



After the Rift




The Rift’s opening brought strange phenomena to the steppe. The night sky became a swirling tapestry of warp‑lit auroras, and travellers reported seeing phantom lights dancing across the plains. Some claimed to hear distant chanting carried on the wind, though no source could be found. The steppe’s storms grew more violent, and entire caravans vanished without a trace. Yet Terra Terminus remains inhabited — its people too stubborn to abandon the land they have walked for generations. In the age of Nihilus, Terra Terminus is the silent frontier, a place where the boundary between reality and the warp feels perilously thin.
