War For Bramnunea

Bastion Mortis — The Silent Fortress of the Fallen



Bastion Mortis is a vast necropolis stretching across rolling hills and fortified terraces. It is the planetary grave‑sanctum, where the honoured dead of the Imperium are interred. Marble ossuaries, statue‑lined avenues, and tomb‑fortresses mark the resting places of heroes, governors, saints, decorated soldiers, and even a few visiting Imperial dignitaries whose deaths occurred on Branunea Secondus.
The necropolis is overseen by the Order of the Silent Vigil, a monastic order sworn to eternal guardianship. Its members walk the necropolis in silent patrols, tending to the tombs, maintaining the eternal flame‑shrines, and recording the deeds of the fallen in endless scrolls of remembrance. Their presence gives the region an air of solemnity — a quiet, reverent stillness broken only by the tolling of memorial bells.
During times of war, Bastion Mortis becomes a place of pilgrimage for regiments preparing to depart from Widow’s Coast. Soldiers kneel before the tombs of their predecessors, seeking courage and guidance. Many leave behind tokens — a knife, a patch, a prayer‑scroll — as offerings to the honoured dead.
The necropolis is more than a graveyard; it is a symbol of continuity, a reminder that every generation of Branuneans stands upon the shoulders of those who came before. In the Northern Province, the past is never forgotten — it is carved in stone, sanctified in ritual, and guarded for all time.



After the Rift




When the Great Rift split the galaxy, Bastion Mortis was plunged into a perpetual half‑light that transformed the necropolis into a place of eerie reverence and renewed purpose. Warp‑charged auroras cast shifting shadows across its tomb‑terraces, and the Order of the Silent Vigil fortified mausoleums and ossuaries into defensive redoubts, turning the resting place of heroes into a bulwark for the living. Reports of flickering eternal flames, whispering winds, and statues shedding condensation were declared miracles by the Ecclesiarchy, signs that the honoured dead stood watch over Branunea Secondus in its isolation. Pilgrims flocked to the necropolis seeking courage, and the Silent Vigil opened ancient sanctums to guide the faithful through the darkness. In the age of Nihilus, Bastion Mortis has become more than a graveyard — it is a fortress of memory, a sanctuary of resolve, and a symbol that even cut off from the Imperium’s light, the dead of Branunea Secondus still guard their world.
