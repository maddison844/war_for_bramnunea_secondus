War For Bramnunea

Aurex Plateaux — A Sacred Plateau Rising from the Sands



The Aurex Plateaux is a colossal, flat‑topped mesa rising from the desert like a titan’s anvil. Its sheer cliffs glow red at sunrise and sunset, earning it the nickname “the Ember Throne.” The plateau’s summit is surprisingly fertile, fed by ancient aquifers and dotted with hardy shrubs, sacred groves, and the ruins of pre‑Imperial settlements. Pilgrims climb the winding paths carved into the cliffs to reach the summit, where they meditate, pray, or seek visions in the thin desert air.
The plateau has long been considered a holy site, though no one agrees on why. Some say it was once a fortress; others claim it was a place of judgement or a beacon for ancient starfarers. Archaeological expeditions have uncovered relics of unknown origin, but the desert’s harshness has prevented deeper exploration. The plateau’s isolation has preserved its mysteries — and its dangers.



After the Rift




When the Rift opened, the Aurex Plateaux became a place of visions. Pilgrims reported seeing lights dancing across the summit, hearing voices in forgotten languages, or experiencing dreams that felt more like memories. The plateau’s red stone began to glow faintly at night, as if reflecting the warp‑lit sky. Some priests believe the plateau is a conduit, amplifying the psychic turbulence of Nihilus. Others fear it is awakening something ancient. In the age of the Rift, the Aurex Plateaux is the crimson sentinel of the desert — watching, waiting, remembering.
