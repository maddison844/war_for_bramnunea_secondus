War For Bramnunea

Central Frontier — Where Fertility Meets the Edge of the Badlands



The Central Frontier is the transitional heart of Branunea Secondus — a broad belt of plains, rolling hills, and river‑cut valleys that separates the fertile Northern Province from the harsher lands to the south and west. It is a region defined by labour, resilience, and quiet pride, where the soil is rich enough to sustain life but demanding enough to shape character. The Frontier has always been the planet’s agricultural backbone, feeding both the hive‑cities and the Imperial tithe, yet it has never enjoyed the prestige of the Northern Province nor the rugged mystique of the Western Marches. Instead, it is a land of steady toil, honest harvests, and communities bound by necessity.
The Frontier’s settlements are older than many records suggest — some dating back to the first waves of colonisation. Its people are known for their practicality, their stubborn independence, and their deep respect for the land. Though overshadowed by the ecclesiastical splendour of the North, the Central Frontier is where the saint’s story truly began, for it was here, at Saint’s End, that Saint Biffobrin fell in battle during the reclamation of the world.



After the Rift




When the Great Rift opened, the Central Frontier became the planet’s lifeline. With off‑world supply chains severed and Administratum oversight gone, the Frontier’s fields and riverlands became the primary source of food for the entire planet. Warp‑charged storms disrupted weather patterns, forcing farmers to adapt quickly or starve. Strange lights danced across the night sky, and some claimed to see ghostly figures walking the fields at dusk. Yet the Frontier endured. Its people tightened their belts, expanded irrigation networks, and worked by torchlight when the auroras drowned out the sun. In the age of Nihilus, the Central Frontier is no longer just the breadbasket — it is the beating stomach of Branunea Secondus.
