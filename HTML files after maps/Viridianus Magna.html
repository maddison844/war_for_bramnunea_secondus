War For Bramnunea

Viridianus Magna — Capital of Branunea Secondus



Viridianus Magna is a marvel of Imperial adaptation. Once a towering hive‑city of manufactoria and smog‑choked spires, it was reshaped centuries ago into a colossal agricultural engine. Its upper levels still pierce the clouds, but instead of industrial smoke, they glow with the green luminescence of hydroponic terraces, nutrient‑vats, and stacked grow‑chambers. Entire hab‑blocks have been hollowed out and converted into vertical farms, their interiors humming with the sound of pumps, nutrient circulators, and artificial suns.
The plains surrounding the hive are carved into vast, geometric fields — regimented rows of grain, grox‑feed, and hardy crops engineered to thrive in the northern climate. Administratum tithe‑clerks refer to Viridianus Magna as “the granary of the Governor’s Throne”, and its output alone fulfils nearly a third of the world’s Imperial tithe. Despite its agrarian focus, the city remains the political heart of Branunea Secondus. The Governor’s Palace rises from the hive’s central plateau, a fortress of marble and adamantine whose banners can be seen for kilometres.



After the Rift


The opening of the Great Rift severed the trade routes and tithe convoys that once linked Viridianus Magna to the wider Imperium. Without off‑world oversight, the hive became self‑sufficient, its hydroponic systems pushed to their limits to feed the planet’s population. The Governor’s Palace transformed into a command citadel, coordinating planetary defence and rationing.
In the absence of Administratum contact, Viridianus Magna’s bureaucrats became priests of practicality — their ledgers treated as sacred texts, their crop yields as offerings to the Emperor. The city’s glow now cuts through the perpetual aurora that hangs above the Rift, a green beacon of survival in the dark. To the people of Branunea Secondus, Viridianus Magna is no longer just the capital — it is the heart that refuses to stop beating.
