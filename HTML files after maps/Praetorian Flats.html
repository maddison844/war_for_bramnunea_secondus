Praetorian Flats — Salt, Silence, and the Weight of the Sun



The Praetorian Flats are a vast expanse of blinding white salt stretching across the northern desert interior. Flat, featureless, and shimmering with heat, the region is one of the most inhospitable places on the planet. The salt crust hides deep sinkholes and brine pools capable of dissolving flesh in minutes. Travel across the Flats requires specialised crawlers or the guidance of the Saltwardens, a nomadic order that has mastered the art of reading subtle shifts in the surface.
The Flats are named for an ancient legend: that a regiment of Praetorian Guard once crossed the desert during the reclamation of the world, losing nearly all their number to heat, thirst, and treacherous terrain. Their bones, it is said, still lie beneath the salt, preserved by the desert’s merciless dryness. Mining enclaves dot the region’s edges, extracting rare minerals and chemical salts used in industry and warfare.



After the Rift




The Rift’s opening caused the Flats to behave strangely. The salt crust began to hum faintly at night, and travellers reported seeing geometric patterns etched into the surface after storms — patterns that vanished by morning. Vox signals refracted unpredictably, creating ghost‑echoes of conversations from days past. Some Saltwardens believe the Flats are “listening” now, though none can say to what. In the age of Nihilus, the Praetorian Flats have become a white mirror, reflecting the galaxy’s madness in ways no one fully understands.
